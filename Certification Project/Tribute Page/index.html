Kazi Nazrul Islam

The national poet of Bangladesh

Here's a time line of Kazi Nazrul Islam's life:

  • 1899 - Born in West Bengal,India
  • 1908 - His fathter died
  • 1910 - Nazrul left the trouph and enrolled at the Searsole Raj High School
  • 1914 - Nazrul studied in the Darirampur
  • 1917 - He joined the British Indian Army
  • 1919 - His first prose work, "Baunduler Stmakahini" was published
  • 1921 - He was engaged to Nargis
  • 1922 - He wrote Bidrohi(The Rebel), which remains his most famous work,winning the admiration of India's literary society for his deccription of a rebel
  • 1923 - 23 January 1923 he was arrested and charged with sedition
  • 1924 - his book "Bisher Bashi" was published
  • 1928 - Nazrul Islam began working as a lyricist, composer, and music director for His Master's Voice Gramophone Company
  • 1939 - Nazrul began working for Calcutta Radio, supervising the production and broadcasting of the station's musical programs
  • 1940 - He returned to journalism in 1940 by working as chief editor for the daily newspaper Nabajug ('New Age'), founded by the Bengali politician A. K. Fazlul Huq
  • 1952 - he was transferred to a psychiatric hospital in Ranchi
  • 1976 - Despite receiving treatment and attention, Nazrul Islam's physical and mental health did not improve; and Nazrul Islam soon died from his long-standing ailments on 29 August 1976
